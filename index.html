ITEM RECOMMENDER

In this project, the requirement was to provide recommendations for items of one of the largest manufacturer in the US followed by identifying the probability of a customer returning in order to help their email marketing campaign.

1. After identifying the time frame for repeating customers and how to handle newly launched items, an Item-Based Collaborative Filtering algorithm with jaccard score as a metric was developed.

2. Since the returning rate of customers were too low, Synthetic Minority Over Sampling Technique (SMOTE) was implemented to balance the data followed by a logistic regression to predict the probability of a customer returning.

3. The results were implemented in a Shiny app and the A/B testing resulted in 2.5% of increase in sales.

DEMAND FORECASTING

The requirement was to project the sales of both newly launched and existing items for one of the leading manufacturer of furniture in the US by providing a month ahead and real time daily updates of the forecasts.

1. For the newly launched items, 12-month forecasts were provided using an ensemble of L2-Regularized regression and a fixed effects model by considering the trend and seasonality observed in items with similar characteristics such color, type, division etc.

2. For the existing items, after identifying new interactive features and basic transformations, a 3-month forecast with real time daily updates was provided using a L2-Regularized regression.

3. Power BI was used to represent and deliver the results

STUDENT PERSISTENCE ANALYSIS

The task was to identify the importance and impact of the factors that play a vital role in the persistence of students into the following term of study

Given the binary nature of the target variable, a logistic regression was developed initially and it was converted into a marginal effects model in order to allow easier interpretation of the coefficients.

The results were represented through a waterfall chart for better visualization

MULTI MODEL FUSION

The idea was to build an automated forecasting tool for providing generic forecasts for any given data set.
In order to provide stable forecasts with sufficient trade-off between bias and variance, several models were developed as follows:

1. Time Series Models: Several Time Series models such as Holt's Winter, ARIMA and TSLM were developed to provide forecasts
2. Regression and it's variants : Different variations in regression including lags, exponential weighted moving average, Lasso, Ridge and Elnet were developed
3. Machine Learning Models: Random Forest and Gradient Boosting was developed in addition to the above mentioned model
4. Non-Negative Least Squares Regression : A constrained regression was developed based on the above obtained results in order to compute the weighted average of the above developed models

CUSTOMER CONVERSION PREDICTION SYSTEM

The requirement was to identify the customers who would subscribe for a term deposit which would help the client in their marketing campaign.

After removal of outliers using the Box-plot and Cook's distance, PCA was executed to reduce the dimensionality in the data and K-means clustering was performed to segment customers and provide the clusters as an independent variable to the model.

After evaluating the individual performance of logistic regression and random forest with respect to F-Score, a weighted ensemble of the 2 models was taken to improve the precision and recall and a SHINY app was developed for real time interactions

AMAZON REVIEW ANALYSIS

PERL based API was used to extract and format the reviews from Amazon website. After basic cleansing of text by removing stop words, special characters, numbers, URL's etc, stemming and suffix dropping was performed.

The cleansing was followed by few interactive plots such as word cloud, word-frequency plot and Association plot.

Finally hierarchial and K-means clustering was performed to segment the reviews followed by a Naive-bayes classifier to estimate the sentiment of the reviews and implemented the solution using SHINY

FANTASY PREMIER LEAGUE ANALYSIS

Built a predictive model to project the points scored by English Premier League players as per the Fantasy Premier League rules and licensed the same to a Draft Fantasy platform

The approach is as follows :

1. After basic cleansing, some of the new interactive features that were introduced are : Opponent difficulty, opposition player difficulty and current form of the player.

2. Multicollinearity among the variables were removed using Variance Inflation Factor (VIF)

3. An ensemble of linear regression and gradient boosting was implemented to provide the points projections with a maximum lead time of three weeks
